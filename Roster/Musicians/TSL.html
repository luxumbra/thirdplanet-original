About the band

Since forming in 2018, hard rock band, TSL (Three shot Limit) of St. Petersburg Fl has already had an impressive digital radio run, which included topping the charts on Boston Rock Radio with their first two singles. TSL would finish the year among the station's top 50 most requested acts of 2020.

TSL was founded by guitarist Jeff Korb, and features the renowned Luis Antonio Alicea on drums and Ross Kevles on bass guitar.

Their most recent EP ‘Irreverent’ is inspired by the rock greats from the 70s to the 90s - “basically anything meant to be played loud with the windows down” (Fireworks Rock and Metal Magazine)

Both ‘Irreverent’ and the debut 2020 EP ‘Fun Little Lies’ have received critical acclaim and were both produced by Peter Klett, founding guitarist of Candlebox.

TSL will be taking the stage beginning in summer of 2021.

Luis Antonio Alicea
Drums

As a Professional touring and recording Drummer, Percussionist and producer, Luis continues to influence the drum world by raising the standards of musicianship. 
Influenced from The Gospel and a musical enriched family, his soul was infused with the blessing of music. Born and raised in Jersey City N.J. Luis started his journey in percussion in 1985. Right from the beginning Alicea was being sought out and requested by big headliners such as Grammy award wining Artist FANIA ALL STARS Richie Ray and Boby Cruz and many others such as Al Di Meola Master Percussionist Gumbi Ortiz. You can see Luis playing with various artists such as Al DI Meola,Julio Iglesias, The Allen Carmen Project, Carl Fisher (Billy Joel ) TÈTI Band, The Electrik Rendezvous, New Groove City, Jeremey Carter Rubber Band and his current band T.S.L.

 Jeff Korb
Guitar

Florida guitarist/songwriter, Jeff Korb, laments he learned to play guitar in the mid/late 80s whenit was more of a full-contact sport and art form of the time. He jokingly reflects, that his favoritesongs were ranked by which ones had the coolest solos.A detour from music into a banking career (life happens) brought Korb a new outlook. Steppingback from seriously playing music for so long and just listening and enjoying, created anenvironment where I was hearing songs with a completely different ear. It took me back toplaying guitar to then writing songs and finally pushing forth with a new band."I found myself listening to songs and wondering how to put myself in the mindset of thesongwriter," he says. "I wanted to think the way they were thinking and understand theircreative process. I wanted to take that knowledge and apply it to the ideas I had. It made mesuch a better musician. I stopped being concerned as a guitar player about how fast I could playa few licks in a guitar solo and focused on creating music and songs people wanted to hear. That was when I stopped being a guy that could play guitar and became a musician that plays guitar."

Ross Kevles
Bass Guitar

Before joining the band Three Shot Limit, Ross Kevles was mostly known for his widevariety of cover band experience. Most notably the cover band Whiskey Straight, whowas sponsored by Fireball, which gained popularity in Virginia Beach circa 2011-2015playing hundreds of performances at every local venue that supported live music. . Rossshows no fear when it comes to assisting in back-up vocals, bringing in his contagiousenergy, and always seems to get the crowd jumping along with him while he's on stage.While serving in the US Navy, for over 20 years, Ross played all over the world bringinghis style and creative process everywhere he played which ranged from dive bars andfestivals to every size concert hall, he has played to every type of crowd . Now residingin the Tampa Bay Area his focus has been on a continued desire to master his craft andfurther his musical development in reading and writing all while performing this neworiginal music. Born and raised in the great city of Philadelphia, Ross was alwaysimmersed by the music that surrounded him. From Motown to Bebop, to Broadway andMetal along with everything in-between, music was always around. His musicalinspirations are vast but some of his favorite bass players are Paul McCartney, John P.Jones, James Jamerson, John Decon, Robert De Leo and Steve Harris, along with so manyother amazing talented low enders
